Museum Night Fever

11.03.2017 7pm - 1am

Gustav Mesmer was born in 1903 and died in 1994, but knew virtually nothing of the 20th century. Placed in a mental institution in Lautertal Valley when he was 26, he focused on his obsessive mission: to create flying machines that would give him freedom.

On this night of 11 March 2017, make Mesmer’s dream live on by creating your own fleet of flying machines to the rhythm of electro music and experimental ambiance.


Participatory workshop:

The collectif 1soir [1 evening group] will invite you to create your flying machines in the span of an evening to supplement the open-ended installation.

Guided tours on Erfgoeddag [Heritage Day]

23.04.2017 14h

The heritage day organised by the Vlaamse Gemeenschapscommissie [Flemish Community Commission] is devoted to the theme of “care” this year.

…Art as care treatment… How is that reflected in the works of the collection of the art et marges musée? We ask the question during a guided tour of the trail of artists who created outside the usual artistic circuit – a discovery of outsider art.

Guided tours of the Gustav Mesmer exhibition + Another world

(an activity of the Friends of the art and margins museum)

07.05.2017

The Dr Guislain Museum and the art et marges musée have one aspect in common: an outsider art collection. In the spring of 2017, they will share another aspect: hosting the works of Gustav Mesmer in their temporary exhibition. We invite you to discover the Gustav Mesmer exhibition in the art et marges musée, and extend your discovery of this artist to Ghent in an exhibition entitled “Een andere wereld” [Another World].

GROUPS AND GUIDED VISITS

Adults and Higher Education

The approach of our guided tours customized for the group and open to a vulnerable public as well is to delve into the work first and then explore the artist and his or her creative context.

Children and teenagers

School and extra-curricular visits, as of 4 years of age, customized for the participants (age, level and type of education, etc.) and the context of the visit. Renewed educational material for each visit stimulates the eye and sensitivities of younger children (kindergarten, primary school), whilst interaction takes pride of place for visits by teenagers.
