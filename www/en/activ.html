BABY POWER !

14.03.2021

Welcome to babies and their parents! The museum has reserved the exhibition just for you! Don't be afraid of the reproving looks of other visitors and let your offspring guide you! A free route to discover according to the desires of toddlers; objects to handle, colourful and sound experiments, an adventure adapted to babies from 0 to 3 years old !

Price : 5€ by participant.

Hic et nunc, variable geometric maps

04.08.2021 5-7 pm

The Art et marges museum exhibits in its Laboratory the results of the cartographic exploration workshops with the artist Jeroen Hollander, a leading artist of the museum's collection. Imaginary, mental, emotional and musical territories ... Outlines are defined with pencils, collages, photographs and masked smiles.

A collaboration between Art et marges musée, l'Autre "lieu" ASBL and KANAL- Centre Pompidou.

Free, on booking

Model your imagination!

04.14.2021 2-4pm

Knead, model and shape an imaginary civilisation by taking inspiration from the work Amalgame and New Dinosaurs by Seyni Awa Camara, François De Jonge and Rémi Lambert. Children and parents are invited to model the statuettes of an imaginary civilization on earth. A workshop where stories are told in clay!

Parents-Kids activity, 4 to 12 years old, on booking, 15€ by person

Spot on! As part of Erfgoeddag

04.24.2021 10-12 am

Heritage Day is an annual cultural event focusing on cultural heritage. Suddently! The lights go out in the Art et marges museum! SPOT ON! and let's play! Projected shadows and light, take up the challenge to draw them! In this workshop, you are both a model and an artist. So put on this crazy hat and sharpen your pencil! Erfgoeddag is an initiative by FARO in collaboration with the heritage communities in Flanders and Brussels and supported by the Flemish government.

On booking, 7 to 12 years old

End of the exhibition Embrasez-vous!

04.25.2021

Join the Cabaret-Concert Tu es ma petite beukenotje in front of the Art et marges museum. French song by Joséphine Dusson at voice and theatrical narration and Asley Neyens at accordion. Joséphine was born in the Marolles 90 years ago, she tells in song the destiny of the women of this popular district of Brussels, bitter-sweet stories about poverty and the world of the night.

A KNEPH initiative for the Erfgoeddag. At 11 am and 3.30 pm (duration: 50 min)

And to make the museum shine all day long, the students of the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in residence at the museum will offer a special live broadcast on the museum's radio station A l'aveuglette. Listen... Can you hear soft music far away? See you on 5th of May for the opening of our next exhibition: Love song!

Free, all day long

Radio A l'aveuglette

Working documents for the radio programme À l'aveuglette The bi-monthly radio show À l'Aveuglette invites you to discover the exhibition Embrasez-vous! with your eyes closed. Fictitious visits, readings of texts, sound experiments and interviews follow one another to experience the museum blindly! It is thought up and proposed by students of the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of Brussels.

The podcast: https://soundcloud.com/a-laveuglette.
For the live podcast, go to our social networks or meet us in front of the museum: it's broadcast in the street!

GROUPS AND GUIDED VISITS

Adults and Higher Education

The approach of our guided tours customized for the group and open to a vulnerable public as well is to delve into the work first and then explore the artist and his or her creative context.

Children and teenagers

School and extra-curricular visits, as of 4 years of age, customized for the participants (age, level and type of education, etc.) and the context of the visit. Renewed educational material for each visit stimulates the eye and sensitivities of younger children (kindergarten, primary school), whilst interaction takes pride of place for visits by teenagers.
