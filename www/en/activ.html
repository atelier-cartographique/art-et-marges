Hic et nunc, variable geometric maps

04.08.2021 5-7 pm

The Art et marges museum is exhibiting in its Laboratory the results of cartographic exploration workshops conducted by the artist-illustrator-printmaker Axel Claes, the advised and caring eye of Julie Guiches, the members of the association l'Autre Lieu and the artist Jeroen Hollander, a major artist in the Art et marges museum’s collection.

Imaginary, mental, affective or urban territories, the outlines are defined with pencils, collages and exchanges around a Riso machine made available at Kanal - Centre Pompidou.

Participants: Vincent, Sanae, Hagen, Gwendoline, Ellen, Yasmin, Marianne with the intervention of the associations Club 55 and Revers under

A collaboration between Art et marges musée, l'Autre "lieu" ASBL and KANAL- Centre Pompidou.

Free of charge, by booking for the 17-18h slot via https://tickets.artetmarges.be/?lang=en and from 18-19h by email via alix.hubermont@artetmarges.be

Model your imagination!

Spot on! As part of Erfgoeddag

04.24.2021 10-12 am

Heritage Day is an annual cultural event focusing on cultural heritage. Suddently! The lights go out in the Art et marges museum! SPOT ON! and let's play! Projected shadows and light, take up the challenge to draw them! In this workshop, you are both a model and an artist. So put on this crazy hat and sharpen your pencil! Erfgoeddag is an initiative by FARO in collaboration with the heritage communities in Flanders and Brussels and supported by the Flemish government.

On booking, 7 to 12 years old, free of charge

Booking : alix.hubermont@artetmarges.be

End of the exhibition Embrasez-vous!

04.25.2021

Come and celebrate with us the end of the exhibition Embrasez-vous!

If you book the 11am or 3pm time slot, you can safely attend the cabaret-performance-sanitary (30 minutes) Tu es ma petite beukenotje. French song by Joséphine Dusson on voice and theatrical narration and Asley Neyens on accordion.

Joséphine was born in the Marolles 90 years ago and tells in song the fate of the women of this working-class district of Brussels, bitter-sweet stories about poverty and the world of the night. An initiative of KNEPH for the Erfgoeddag.

And to keep the whole day going, the students of the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in residence at the museum will be offering a special live broadcast on the museum radio station A l'aveuglette.

Listen carefully... Can't you hear the music in the distance? See you on 6 May for the opening of our next exhibition: Love song !

Free, on booking

Booking : https://tickets.artetmarges.be/?lang=en

Radio A l'aveuglette

Working documents for the radio programme À l'aveuglette The bi-monthly radio show À l'Aveuglette invites you to discover the exhibition Embrasez-vous! with your eyes closed. Fictitious visits, readings of texts, sound experiments and interviews follow one another to experience the museum blindly! It is thought up and proposed by students of the Royal Academy of Fine Arts of Brussels.

The podcast: https://soundcloud.com/a-laveuglette.

GROUPS AND GUIDED VISITS

Adults and Higher Education

The approach of our guided tours customized for the group and open to a vulnerable public as well is to delve into the work first and then explore the artist and his or her creative context.

Children and teenagers

School and extra-curricular visits, as of 4 years of age, customized for the participants (age, level and type of education, etc.) and the context of the visit. Renewed educational material for each visit stimulates the eye and sensitivities of younger children (kindergarten, primary school), whilst interaction takes pride of place for visits by teenagers.
